## Gap analysis

- **Keyword gap.** Aggregators blanket the head terms with templated city/date pages BDQ can't match
  in volume. BDQ's realistic targets: **branded** + **curated/premium long-tail** (e.g. "curated
  night market Vadodara", "premium lifestyle festival Vadodara") + the scaffolded informational hubs.
- **Content gap.** Aggregators have thousands of indexed pages; BDQ has ~15. The two content hubs
  (this pass) are a start; sustained content is needed (`geo-audit.md`).
- **Schema gap — now closed (and an edge).** Most local/direct rivals (incl. typical Wix/Weebly flea
  sites like The White Flea's stack) ship weak or no Event schema. BDQ now emits full Event + Offer +
  Organization + Breadcrumb + FAQ JSON-LD — **a genuine technical advantage** for rich results + AI.
- **Authority gap — the real moat.** Aggregators have years of backlinks and domain authority. A new
  single-brand domain cannot outrank them on head terms short-term. **This is the binding constraint.**
- **Distribution gap.** Insider/District/BMS bundle discovery + checkout + an app. BDQ owns its
  checkout but has no discovery distribution → must **list on the aggregators**, not fight them.

## Strategic takeaway

**Don't try to outrank aggregators on head terms — join them, and beat the direct rival on quality.**

1. List BDQ on every aggregator above (free discovery + citations + backlinks).
2. Out-execute **The White Flea** on the queries that convert: branded, "curated/premium", and the
   informational hubs — where superior schema, page experience, and a tighter brand story win.
3. Win the **AI-citation game** early (`geo-audit.md`) — it's the least authority-gated channel.
